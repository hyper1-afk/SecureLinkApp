🛡️ Online Safety Tips

Follow these essential tips to protect yourself from online threats

1

Always Verify Before You Click

Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. If it looks suspicious or doesn't match the expected domain, don't click it.

2

Check for HTTPS

Look for the padlock icon and "https://" in the URL. Legitimate websites handling sensitive data always use secure connections.

3

Beware of Urgency Tactics

Scammers often create a false sense of urgency. Be suspicious of messages claiming your account will be closed or you'll miss a deal.

4

Watch for Spelling Errors

Phishing sites often use misspelled domain names like "arnazon.com" or "paypa1.com". Always double-check the spelling.

5

Don't Trust Shortened Links

URL shorteners can hide malicious destinations. Use SecureLink to verify shortened URLs before visiting them.

6

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your browser, operating system, and antivirus software to protect against known vulnerabilities.

7

Use Unique Passwords

Never reuse passwords across multiple sites. If one site is breached, attackers can access all your accounts.

8

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts. Even if someone steals your password, they can't access your account.
