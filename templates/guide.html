Verifying Links

The core feature of SecureLink is our powerful link verification system. Here's how to use it effectively:

How to Verify a Link

  1. Copy the suspicious link - Right-click on a link and select "Copy link address" (don't click the link itself!)
  2. Paste it into SecureLink - Go to the main page and paste the URL in the verification box
  3. Click "Verify Link" - Our AI will analyze the link in seconds
  4. Review the results - Check the risk score and any warnings before deciding to visit the link
Pro Tip: You can verify links from emails, text messages, social media, or anywhere else. When in doubt, always check first!

What We Analyze

  • Domain reputation - Is this domain known for malicious activity?
  • SSL certificate - Does the site have valid security certificates?
  • URL patterns - Does the URL contain suspicious patterns or misspellings?
  • Redirect chains - Does the link redirect through suspicious servers?
  • Phishing indicators - Does the page try to impersonate a legitimate site?
  • Malware databases - Is this URL flagged in known threat databases?

Understanding Results

After verification, you'll receive a detailed analysis. Here's how to interpret the results:

Risk Levels

Each link receives a risk score from 0-100%, categorized as follows:

Safe (0-20%) Low Risk (21-40%) Medium (41-60%) High Risk (61-80%) Critical (81-100%)

What Each Level Means

  • Safe: The link appears to be legitimate. You can likely proceed safely.
  • Low Risk: Minor concerns detected, but generally safe. Exercise normal caution.
  • Medium Risk: Some suspicious indicators found. Verify the source before clicking.
  • High Risk: Multiple warning signs detected. Avoid unless you're certain of the source.
  • Critical: Confirmed or highly suspected malicious link. Do not visit!
Warning: Even "Safe" results don't guarantee 100% safety. New threats emerge constantly. Always use your judgment and consider the context of where you received the link.

Security Best Practices

Protect yourself online with these essential security tips:

Link Safety

  • Hover before clicking - Check where a link actually goes before clicking
  • Be wary of shortened URLs - Services like bit.ly can hide malicious destinations
  • Check for HTTPS - Secure sites should have a padlock icon
  • Watch for misspellings - "amaz0n.com" is not "amazon.com"
  • Verify unexpected links - Even if from a known contact, their account may be compromised

Email Safety

  • Don't trust urgent requests - Scammers create false urgency
  • Verify sender addresses - Check the actual email address, not just the display name
  • Never download unexpected attachments - These may contain malware
  • When in doubt, verify - Contact the company directly through their official website
Remember: Legitimate companies will never ask for your password, Social Security number, or credit card details via email or text message.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SecureLink free to use?

Yes! Basic link verification is completely free. Pro features like email monitoring and weekly reports require a subscription.

How accurate is the link analysis?

Our AI-powered analysis is highly accurate, combining multiple threat databases and real-time analysis. However, no system is 100% perfect. New threats emerge constantly, so always use caution.

Do you store my email content?

No. We only scan for links and analyze them. Your actual email content is never stored or read by our team.

What if a safe link is marked as dangerous?

False positives can occasionally occur. If you believe a link was incorrectly flagged, please report it through the IT support system and our team will review it.

Can I use SecureLink on mobile?

Yes! SecureLink is fully responsive and works on smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers.

How do I cancel my subscription?

You can cancel anytime from your Profile page under Subscription settings. You'll retain access until the end of your billing period.

Is my data secure?

Absolutely. We use industry-standard encryption for all data. Your passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text.
